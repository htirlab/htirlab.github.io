Adolescent Online Safety

Adolescent online safety research focuses specifically on understanding and addressing the risks and challenges that adolescents face in their online activities. It aims to explore the unique vulnerabilities and needs of young people in the digital world and develop strategies to promote their well-being and safety. Researchers investigate issues such as cyberbullying, online grooming, sexting, privacy concerns, and the impact of excessive screen time on mental health. The goal of adolescent online safety research is to provide evidence-based recommendations, educational resources, and policy guidance to empower young people, parents, educators, and policymakers in creating a safer online environment for adolescents.

Usable Privacy and Security

Usable Privacy and Security (UP&S) research focuses on studying and improving the usability and effectiveness of privacy and security measures in digital systems. It combines principles from human-computer interaction (HCI), psychology, and computer security to design user-friendly solutions that protect users' privacy and enhance their security online. Researchers investigate how individuals perceive and interact with privacy and security mechanisms, identify usability challenges, and propose design guidelines and interventions to bridge the gap between users' mental models and system requirements. The goal of UP&S research is to develop practical and user-centric solutions that strike a balance between protecting sensitive information and providing a seamless and intuitive user experience
